The Short Answer

Alireza Rahmani Khalili, in brief

Alireza Rahmani Khalili builds systems that have to work in production, not just look correct in demos. His work focuses on the failure modes most teams underestimate: silent degradation, stale data, unreliable pipelines, and architecture that breaks once real users arrive.

He is a Principal Software Engineer specializing in production-grade AI systems, distributed backend architecture, real-world RAG pipelines, and data platforms that operate under scale, partial failure, and constant change.

His engineering lens is simple: if a system only works in theory, it does not work. Good architecture must degrade gracefully, remain observable, and keep delivering value when assumptions break, because assumptions always break. Humanity keeps rediscovering this and calling it innovation.

He is currently at RoomVu, where he works on MLS data infrastructure and AI pipeline systems. Before that, he held engineering roles at Worksome in Copenhagen, Selected Geeks in Brighton, and earlier at SnappMarket and Digikala, one of the largest e-commerce platforms in the Middle East.

What He Does

Technical Focus

Most AI systems do not fail loudly. They decay. Retrieval quality drifts, embeddings become stale, data pipelines fall behind reality, and the output keeps looking plausible long after it has stopped being reliable.

Alireza’s core expertise is in distributed systems engineering: designing systems that handle high throughput, stay consistent under pressure, and degrade gracefully when things go wrong. He has hands-on experience with production RAG pipelines, including hybrid retrieval, reranking, embedding migration, evaluation, and data freshness problems.

He approaches architecture through Domain-Driven Design, using bounded contexts, ownership boundaries, and domain language to keep software aligned with the business instead of letting the codebase become a museum of accidental decisions.

AI systems: RAG pipelines, retrieval quality, embedding drift, evaluation, and production behavior.
Backend systems: high-throughput ingestion, synchronization, idempotency, and operational reliability.
Architecture: Domain-Driven Design, bounded contexts, service boundaries, and system design trade-offs.
